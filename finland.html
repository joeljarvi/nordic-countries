Finland

Here’s a version of the text simplified for a five-year-old: --- Finland is a country in Northern Europe. It’s right next to Sweden, Norway, and Russia. To the west is the Gulf of Bothnia, and to the south is the Gulf of Finland, with Estonia across from it. Finland is quite big, covering a lot of land, and has about 5.6 million people living there. The biggest city is Helsinki. Most people in Finland speak Finnish, and some also speak Swedish. The country is known for having lots of forests and lakes—over 180,000 lakes! The weather in Finland can be cold and snowy, especially in the north. People have lived in Finland for a very long time. It was settled after the Ice Age around 9000 BC. Finland was part of Sweden for a long time and then became part of the Russian Empire in 1809. After that, Finland worked hard to become independent, which it did in 1917. Finland had some tough times during World War II but managed to stay independent. After the war, Finland grew quickly and became a very modern and wealthy country. It became part of the European Union in 1995 and joined NATO in 2023. Finland is known for having great schools, a strong economy, and a high quality of life.
