Denmark

Denmark is a country in Northern Europe where lots of people live. It has almost 6 million people, and many of them live in the big city called Copenhagen. Denmark also has two special places called the Faroe Islands and Greenland. These places are far out in the North Atlantic Ocean. Denmark is the southernmost of the Scandinavian countries and is close to Sweden, Norway, and Germany. Denmark has a lot of islands—more than 1,400! Some are big and some are small. The biggest island is called Zealand, where Copenhagen is. Other big islands are Funen and Amager. Denmark has flat land, sandy beaches, and a nice, mild climate. A long time ago, in the 8th century, Denmark was a powerful country with big ships. It joined with Norway and Sweden a long time ago to form something called the Kalmar Union, but they separated later. Denmark fought some wars and changed how it was run in the 1800s, which made it a modern country with good social programs. During World War I, Denmark stayed neutral, but in World War II, Germany invaded it quickly. After the war, Denmark was freed, and in 1973, it joined the European Union. Denmark is known for being a very good place to live and was the first country to say same-sex partnerships are okay. Denmark is a member of many important groups like NATO and the United Nations, and it works closely with its Scandinavian neighbors. People often look at Denmark as a great example of how to run a country well.
