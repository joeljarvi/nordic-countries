Norway

Norway is a country in Northern Europe, on the Scandinavian Peninsula. It has about 5.5 million people living there. The biggest city is Oslo. Norway also has some special places like the Arctic island called Jan Mayen and a group of islands called Svalbard. There’s another island called Bouvet Island, but it’s not really part of Norway, and Norway also thinks it owns some land in Antarctica. Norway is big and has lots of coastlines by the ocean. It shares borders with Sweden, Finland, and Russia. The country is ruled by a king named Harald V and a leader named Jonas Gahr Støre. Norway has a special way of running things with rules from 1814, and it’s been a country for a really long time—over 1,150 years! A long time ago, Norway was part of Denmark and then Sweden before it became its own country. During the big wars, Norway stayed neutral at first but was taken over by Germany in World War II. Norway is divided into counties and smaller areas called municipalities. The Sámi people, who live there, have their own group to help make decisions about their land. Norway is friends with many countries and is part of important groups like the United Nations and NATO. It also shares borders with Sweden, Finland, and Russia. Norway takes care of its people with health care and social services. It has a lot of oil and gas, which helps it earn a lot of money. Norway is very rich and has the biggest money pile in the world, worth about $1.3 trillion!
