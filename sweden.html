Sweden

Sweden is a big country in Northern Europe on the Scandinavian Peninsula. It’s next to Norway and Finland. It’s the largest of the Nordic countries and the fifth-largest country in Europe. The capital city of Sweden is Stockholm, and about 10.6 million people live there. Most people live in cities in the middle and southern parts of the country. Sweden has been around for a very long time, since about 12,000 BC. A long time ago, people from Sweden were known as the Norsemen. Sweden became a united country in the late 10th century. In 1397, Sweden joined with Norway and Denmark to form a group called the Kalmar Union but left in 1523. Sweden became a powerful country and controlled a lot of land around the Baltic Sea. Sweden lost some of its land over the years, including Finland, which is now part of Russia. Sweden was last in a war in 1814 when it joined with Norway for a while, but they went their separate ways in 1905. Sweden is a very developed and happy country. It has a king and a parliament that makes the laws. Sweden provides free health care and education to everyone. It’s known for having a high quality of life and is very fair to everyone. Sweden joined the European Union in 1995 and NATO in 2024. It’s also part of many other important groups like the United Nations and the Schengen Area.
